Why Are We Using This Model Organism

What is a model organism?

An organism that is similar, both physiologically and genetically, to the study organism but easier to study.

Why are they used?

They allow you to investigate and understand processes in the simpler model organism, which can then be applied to the study organism.

Why we aren't using CSFB

  • It is a quarantine pest due to its insecticide resistance
  • UK legislation limits their supply
  • Complex, long life-cycle
  • Cryptic larval stages since they grow inside the stem
  • Summer aestivation
  • Expensive
  • Diet is specific to OSR, hard to replicate under laboratory conditions

Why we chose the red flour beetle (Tribolium castaneum)

  • Closely related

  • Its genome is fully sequenced
  • Enables cheap, high-throughput testing
  • Easy to rear using laboratory conditions and a flour-based diet
  • Short lifecycle producing large numbers

Thus, the red flour beetle is a widely accepted model organism for CSFB and coleoptera more broadly.
