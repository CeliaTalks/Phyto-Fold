Basics Design a novel protein binder to a naturally occurring CSFB receptor to disrupt CSFB pest action, using AI protein design technology Protein A Đ the naturally occurring receptor within CSFB Protein B Đ the protein binder that we are designing to bind to protein A - The interaction between protein A and B will disrupt CSFB pest-action // more detailed steps Step 1From extant literature we have found a novel molecule crucial to CSFB activity and that of other pests, with no homology found in pollinators or humans.  ** This molecule will serve as the receptor in our workflow and be referred to as protein A. Step 2Using characterised crystal structures and AlphaFold predictions we will rent cloud GPU computing to run BindCraft (de novo protein design software) to produce a large panel of potential protein binders (protein B). Step 3 The panel of potential protein B binders will be filtered and refined. Then sent to CRO to run binding affinity tests. Step 4 Binders (protein B) with high affinity to the receptor (protein A) will be optimised to increase binding affinity. Then expressed at scale for insect assay testing
